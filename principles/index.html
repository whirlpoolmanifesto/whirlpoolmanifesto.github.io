Principles of Whirlpool Software Development

We follow these principles:

  1. Communication should almost always be asynchronous. Meetings should be avoided whenever possible.
  2. Effectiveness of large meetings should be judged by the attendees' standard deviation towards the ends of the emotional spectrum. Anticipate and embrace heated debates. Strive for inspired jubilee. Be wary of muted tones - oft indicators of time wasted.
  3. Working too hard, for too long, precedes burnout; plan accordingly.
  4. A blunt smack of honesty to the face is better than a sharp knife of deceit to the back.
  5. Recognition, any recognition, goes a long way. After all, some people will work for as little as shiny stars on a website - so click the thumbs up, throw some high fives, and don’t forget to pay well.
  6. Understand your data. Keep it clean, treat it with respect - treat it like your code - it is your code.
  7. Delivering requirements pays bills. Good design and architecture pays dividends.
  8. Most projects will benefit from a benevolent dictator, but don’t be afraid to fork them.
  9. Adaptation is natural - embrace change. Never stop learning and always refine your work when time permits.
  10. Embrace the outliers; the deviators; the delinquents. They are the keys to success - but you’ll have to find the right keyhole.
  11. Leverage the larger software ecosystem as much as possible, but remember to be eco-friendly - give back, be sustainable.
